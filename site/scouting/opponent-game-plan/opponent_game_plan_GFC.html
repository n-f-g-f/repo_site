Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Know the first reference: B. Carter is a movement shooter, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: B. Carter, D. Runge are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are B. Carter and Q. Eyobo; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with B. Carter, D. Runge, and R. Guerreiro; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from B. Lisboa and Q. Eyobo; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially B. Carter, Q. Eyobo, and B. Lisboa; force them away from preferred areas.
