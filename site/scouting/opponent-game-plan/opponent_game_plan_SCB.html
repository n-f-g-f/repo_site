Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Control the rhythm: they are more comfortable in a controlled half-court game, so do not let them play cleanly into structure.
  • Stay connected to spacing: their perimeter volume can bend the floor if your help and recovery are sloppy.
  • Load into D. Blaylock: he is the main organizer, so make secondary creators finish possessions.
  • Closeout discipline matters: C. Sa, D. Blaylock are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are D. Blaylock and C. Cardoso; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with C. Sa, D. Blaylock, and C. Cardoso; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from S. Fofana and R. Monteiro; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially D. Blaylock, C. Cardoso, and R. Monteiro; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: C. Cardoso | D. Blaylock | J. Bingham II | R. Monteiro | S. Fofana.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: B. Robinson | C. Cardoso | D. Blaylock | R. Monteiro | S. Fofana.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: C. Cardoso | D. Blaylock and C. Cardoso | D. Blaylock | S. Fofana.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Offensive rebounding is around league average; box-outs matter but this is not their main edge.
  • Do not lose C. Sa and D. Blaylock in help-and-recover situations.
