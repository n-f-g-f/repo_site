Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Set the tempo early: if you let them run into flow, the game can speed up on their terms.
  • Do not overreact to the arc: make them finish over traffic instead of giving up easy paint comfort.
  • Know the first reference: C. Jenkins is a movement shooter, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: J. Sangha, C. Jenkins are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • C. Jenkins is the first offensive reference and plays as a movement shooter.
  • Above-average pace; expect a game that can speed up if you allow early flow.
  • Below-average assist profile; more possessions end through direct creation than collective flow.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Closeout priority starts with J. Sangha, C. Jenkins, F. Gewert, and P. Pinto; these are the names most likely to punish late help.
  • Manageable perimeter volume comes from S. Peek, J. Baker, B. Araujo, S. Verde, and P. Bastos; contest honestly, but this is lower-cost offense.
  • Less three-point dependent than league average; more of their offense is built away from pure perimeter volume.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Restricted Area; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially S. Peek, M. Jacobs, and J. Sangha; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: B. Araujo | C. Jenkins | J. Sangha | M. Jacobs | S. Peek.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: B. Araujo | C. Jenkins | J. Sangha | S. Peek | S. Verde.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Offensive rebounding is around league average; box-outs matter but this is not their main edge.
  • Do not lose J. Sangha and C. Jenkins in help-and-recover situations.
