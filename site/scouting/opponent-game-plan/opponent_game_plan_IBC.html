Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Finish possessions: the first stop is not enough against this team because the offensive glass is a real source of extra points.
  • Do not overreact to the arc: make them finish over traffic instead of giving up easy paint comfort.
  • Know the first reference: K. Kennedy is a primary scorer, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are K. Kennedy and A. Silva; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with A. Silva, T. Barnes, and L. Silva; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from K. Kennedy and F. Thomas; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Paint Non-RA; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially K. Kennedy, R. Jones, and F. Lima; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: A. Silva | K. Kennedy | N. Lane | R. Jones | T. Barnes.
