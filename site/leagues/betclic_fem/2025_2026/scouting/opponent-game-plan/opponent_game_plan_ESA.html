How They Play

Tempo
Below-average pace; they are more comfortable when the game stays organized in the half court.
Ball Movement

Offensive Glass

Turnovers

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Control the rhythm: they are more comfortable in a controlled half-court game, so do not let them play cleanly into structure.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Know the first reference: M. Burns is a inside scorer, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: L. Ferreira, M. Umabano are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are M. Umabano and M. Burns; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with L. Ferreira, M. Umabano, and F. Rosa; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from M. Costa Robalo and M. Burns; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Paint Non-RA; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially M. Umabano, M. Costa Robalo, and M. Burns; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: L. Park | M. Burns | M. Costa Robalo | M. Oliveira | M. Umabano.

Defensive Emphasis
