How They Play

Tempo
Below-average pace; they are more comfortable when the game stays organized in the half court.
Ball Movement
Below-average assist profile; more possessions end through direct creation than collective flow.
Offensive Glass
Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
Turnovers
Better-than-average ball security; they do not hand away many live possessions.
Physicality
Lower foul volume than league average; this is not a team that wants constant contact chaos.
Shot Mix
Perimeter volume is close to league average; shot mix alone does not define them.

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Control the rhythm: they are more comfortable in a controlled half-court game, so do not let them play cleanly into structure.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Know the first reference: M. Ewuosho is a spot-up shooter, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: R. Mendes, S. Santis are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are M. Ewuosho and E. Shepherd; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with R. Mendes, S. Santis, and M. Ewuosho; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from T. Evans and M. Monteiro; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially T. Toney, M. Ewuosho, and T. Evans; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: E. Shepherd | M. Ewuosho | S. Santis | T. Evans | T. Toney.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: E. Shepherd | G. Shephard | M. Ewuosho | T. Evans | T. Toney.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: E. Shepherd | M. Ewuosho and E. Shepherd | M. Ewuosho | T. Toney.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
  • Do not lose R. Mendes and S. Santis in help-and-recover situations.
