How They Play

Tempo
Below-average pace; they are more comfortable when the game stays organized in the half court.
Ball Movement
Above-average assist profile; expect cleaner passing sequences and more extra-pass possessions.
Offensive Glass
Offensive rebounding is around league average; box-outs matter but this is not their main edge.
Turnovers
Turnover profile is close to league average; pressure helps, but it is not the whole plan.
Physicality
Lower foul volume than league average; this is not a team that wants constant contact chaos.
Shot Mix
Above-average three-point volume; spacing discipline and clean closeouts matter.

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Control the rhythm: they are more comfortable in a controlled half-court game, so do not let them play cleanly into structure.
  • Stay connected to spacing: their perimeter volume can bend the floor if your help and recovery are sloppy.
  • Know the first reference: D. Blaylock is a movement shooter, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: C. Cardoso, D. Blaylock are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Offense

  • The main offensive references are D. Blaylock and C. Cardoso; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with C. Cardoso, D. Blaylock, and B. Robinson; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from S. Fofana and R. Monteiro; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially D. Blaylock, C. Cardoso, and R. Monteiro; force them away from preferred areas.
