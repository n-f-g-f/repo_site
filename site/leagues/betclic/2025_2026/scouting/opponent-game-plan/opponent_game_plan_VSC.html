How They Play

Tempo
Above-average pace; expect a game that can speed up if you allow early flow.
Ball Movement
Below-average assist profile; more possessions end through direct creation than collective flow.
Offensive Glass
Second-chance threat; the first stop is not enough against this team.
Turnovers
Loose enough with the ball to be pressured; there are possessions to steal here.
Physicality
Foul profile sits near league average; physicality is part of the game, but not their defining trait.
Shot Mix
Less three-point dependent than league average; more of their offense is built away from pure perimeter volume.

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Set the tempo early: if you let them run into flow, the game can speed up on their terms.
  • Finish possessions: the first stop is not enough against this team because the offensive glass is a real source of extra points.
  • Do not overreact to the arc: make them finish over traffic instead of giving up easy paint comfort.
  • Load into C. Jenkins: he is the main organizer, so make secondary creators finish possessions.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are S. Peek and J. Sangha; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with P. Pinto, F. Gewert, and C. Jenkins; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from S. Peek and J. Sangha; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Restricted Area; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially S. Peek, M. Jacobs, and J. Sangha; force them away from preferred areas.

Units
