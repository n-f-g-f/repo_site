Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Control the rhythm: they are more comfortable in a controlled half-court game, so do not let them play cleanly into structure.
  • Finish possessions: the first stop is not enough against this team because the offensive glass is a real source of extra points.
  • Stay connected to spacing: their perimeter volume can bend the floor if your help and recovery are sloppy.
  • Know the first reference: B. Johns is a paint finisher, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are F. Amarante and B. Johns; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with D. Ventura, M. Bowman, and M. Morgan; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from F. Amarante and S. Swenson; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially M. Morgan, B. Johns, and S. Swenson; force them away from preferred areas.

Units
