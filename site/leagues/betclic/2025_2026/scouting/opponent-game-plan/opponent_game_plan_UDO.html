Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Know the first reference: J. Boeheim is a inside scorer, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: J. Sibert, M. McIntosh are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are J. Boeheim and M. McIntosh; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with J. Sibert, M. McIntosh, and J. Embalo; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from J. Boeheim and D. Araujo; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially M. McIntosh, J. Sibert, and J. Boeheim; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: E. Timberlake JR | J. Barbosa | J. Boeheim | J. Sibert | M. McIntosh.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: A. Bessa | E. Timberlake JR | J. Boeheim | J. Sibert | M. McIntosh.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: J. Boeheim | J. Sibert and J. Boeheim | J. Sibert | M. McIntosh.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
  • Do not lose J. Sibert and M. McIntosh in help-and-recover situations.
