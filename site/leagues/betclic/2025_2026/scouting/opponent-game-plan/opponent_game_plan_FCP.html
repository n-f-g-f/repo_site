How They Play

Tempo
League-average pace; rhythm control matters more than pure speed against them.
Ball Movement
Above-average assist profile; expect cleaner passing sequences and more extra-pass possessions.
Offensive Glass
Offensive rebounding is around league average; box-outs matter but this is not their main edge.
Turnovers
Better-than-average ball security; they do not hand away many live possessions.
Physicality
Foul profile sits near league average; physicality is part of the game, but not their defining trait.
Shot Mix
Above-average three-point volume; spacing discipline and clean closeouts matter.

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Stay connected to spacing: their perimeter volume can bend the floor if your help and recovery are sloppy.
  • Know the first reference: C. Hudson is a paint finisher, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: J. Guerreiro, V. Voytso are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are J. Dunn and W. Washpun; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with J. Guerreiro, V. Voytso, and J. Riley; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from R. Beran JR and W. Washpun; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially J. Dunn, W. Washpun, and J. Davis; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: C. Hudson | J. Dunn | M. Queiroz | R. Beran JR | W. Washpun.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: J. Dunn | W. Washpun and J. Davis | J. Dunn | W. Washpun.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Offensive rebounding is around league average; box-outs matter but this is not their main edge.
  • Do not lose J. Guerreiro and V. Voytso in help-and-recover situations.
