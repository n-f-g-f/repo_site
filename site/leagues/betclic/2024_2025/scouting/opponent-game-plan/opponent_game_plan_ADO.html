Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Know the first reference: D. Blaylock is a movement shooter, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: R. Woods, D. Blaylock are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are A. Thompson and R. Woods; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with R. Woods, D. Blaylock, and L. Rahama; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from N. Morais and G. Teixeira; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially D. Blaylock, R. Woods, and A. Thompson; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: A. Thompson | D. Blaylock | I. Pavicevic | L. Rahama | R. Woods.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: A. Thompson | D. Blaylock | J. Silva | R. Soeiro | R. Woods.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: A. Thompson | R. Woods and A. Thompson | D. Blaylock | R. Woods.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
  • Do not lose R. Woods and D. Blaylock in help-and-recover situations.
