Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Finish possessions: the first stop is not enough against this team because the offensive glass is a real source of extra points.
  • Know the first reference: R. Marble is a movement shooter, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: M. Landis, X. Williams are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are T. Douglas and M. Landis; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with M. Landis, X. Williams, and R. Marble; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from T. Douglas and T. Omlid; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially M. Landis, T. Douglas, and P. Fayne II; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: M. Landis | M. Queiroz | R. Marble | T. Douglas | X. Williams.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: M. Landis | P. Fayne II | R. Marble | T. Douglas | X. Williams.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: M. Landis | X. Williams and M. Landis | T. Douglas | X. Williams.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Second-chance threat; the first stop is not enough against this team.
  • Do not lose M. Landis and X. Williams in help-and-recover situations.
