Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Set the tempo early: if you let them run into flow, the game can speed up on their terms.
  • Finish possessions: the first stop is not enough against this team because the offensive glass is a real source of extra points.
  • Closeout discipline matters: D. Blaylock are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with D. Blaylock; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from S. Fofana and C. Cardoso; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially D. Blaylock and R. Monteiro; force them away from preferred areas.

Defensive Emphasis
