Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Load into T. Toney: he is the main organizer, so make secondary creators finish possessions.
  • Closeout discipline matters: B. Alston, M. Diggins JR are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are T. Toney and M. Diggins JR; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with B. Alston, M. Diggins JR, and T. Toney; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from D. Tillman and M. Monteiro; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially T. Toney, D. Tillman, and M. Diggins JR; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: B. Alston | D. Tillman | M. Diggins JR | M. Monteiro | T. Toney.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: D. Tillman | E. Shepherd | M. Diggins JR | M. Monteiro | T. Toney.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: M. Diggins JR | T. Toney and D. Tillman | M. Diggins JR | T. Toney.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
  • Do not lose B. Alston and M. Diggins JR in help-and-recover situations.
