Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Control the rhythm: they are more comfortable in a controlled half-court game, so do not let them play cleanly into structure.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Know the first reference: R. Embo is a spot-up shooter, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: S. Suggs, N. Arsenopoulos are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are S. Robinson and J. Rodrigues; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with S. Suggs, N. Arsenopoulos, and R. Embo; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from V. Tahvanainem and S. Robinson; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially N. Arsenopoulos, C. Trapp JR, and S. Robinson; force them away from preferred areas.
