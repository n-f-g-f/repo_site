How They Play

Tempo
Below-average pace; they are more comfortable when the game stays organized in the half court.
Ball Movement
Below-average assist profile; more possessions end through direct creation than collective flow.
Offensive Glass
Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
Turnovers
Turnover profile is close to league average; pressure helps, but it is not the whole plan.
Physicality
Lower foul volume than league average; this is not a team that wants constant contact chaos.
Shot Mix
Perimeter volume is close to league average; shot mix alone does not define them.

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Control the rhythm: they are more comfortable in a controlled half-court game, so do not let them play cleanly into structure.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Know the first reference: T. Arikawe is a inside scorer, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: A. Iyaye, D. Goncalves are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are M. Bastos and D. Goncalves; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with A. Iyaye, D. Goncalves, and M. Terry; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from V. Banaru and M. Bastos; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Paint Non-RA; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially D. Goncalves, V. Banaru, and T. Arikawe; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: A. Iyaye | D. Goncalves | M. Bastos | T. Arikawe | V. Banaru.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: A. Iyaye | M. Bastos | S. Quissanga | T. Arikawe | V. Banaru.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: D. Goncalves | M. Bastos and D. Goncalves | M. Bastos | V. Banaru.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
  • Do not lose A. Iyaye and D. Goncalves in help-and-recover situations.
