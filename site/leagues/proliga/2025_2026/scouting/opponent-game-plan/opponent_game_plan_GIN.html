How They Play

Tempo
League-average pace; rhythm control matters more than pure speed against them.
Ball Movement
Ball movement is close to league average; they can share it but are not purely pass-first.
Offensive Glass
Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
Turnovers
Turnover profile is close to league average; pressure helps, but it is not the whole plan.
Physicality
Lower foul volume than league average; this is not a team that wants constant contact chaos.
Shot Mix
Less three-point dependent than league average; more of their offense is built away from pure perimeter volume.

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Do not overreact to the arc: make them finish over traffic instead of giving up easy paint comfort.
  • Load into J. McClary: he is the main organizer, so make secondary creators finish possessions.

Scouting Notes

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are A. Akoi and J. McClary; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with J. McClary, E. Fortes, and B. Neves; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from A. Akoi and A. Mendes; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Paint Non-RA; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially J. McClary, A. Howell, and A. Akoi; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: A. Akoi | A. Howell | A. Mendes | E. Fortes | J. McClary.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: A. Akoi | A. Howell | E. Fortes | G. Barbosa | J. McClary.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: A. Akoi | J. McClary and A. Akoi | A. Howell | J. McClary.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
  • Do not lose J. McClary and E. Fortes in help-and-recover situations.
