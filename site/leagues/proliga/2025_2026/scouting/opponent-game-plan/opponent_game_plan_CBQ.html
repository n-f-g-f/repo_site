Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Control the rhythm: they are more comfortable in a controlled half-court game, so do not let them play cleanly into structure.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Stay connected to spacing: their perimeter volume can bend the floor if your help and recovery are sloppy.
  • Know the first reference: B. Newsom is a inside scorer, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are D. Silva and T. Martins; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with D. Westbrook, M. Carmine, and G. Gogua; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from T. Martins and G. Gogua; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially B. Newsom, T. Martins, and D. Westbrook; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: B. Newsom | D. Silva | D. Westbrook | G. Gogua | T. Martins.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: B. Newsom | D. Silva | D. Westbrook | G. Gogua | T. Martins.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: D. Silva | T. Martins and B. Newsom | D. Silva | D. Westbrook.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
  • Do not lose D. Westbrook and M. Carmine in help-and-recover situations.
