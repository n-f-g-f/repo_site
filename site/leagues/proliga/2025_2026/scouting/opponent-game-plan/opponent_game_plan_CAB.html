How They Play

Tempo
League-average pace; rhythm control matters more than pure speed against them.
Ball Movement
Above-average assist profile; expect cleaner passing sequences and more extra-pass possessions.
Offensive Glass
Offensive rebounding is around league average; box-outs matter but this is not their main edge.
Turnovers
Better-than-average ball security; they do not hand away many live possessions.
Physicality
Above-average foul volume; contact pressure and discipline both matter in this matchup.
Shot Mix
Above-average three-point volume; spacing discipline and clean closeouts matter.

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Stay connected to spacing: their perimeter volume can bend the floor if your help and recovery are sloppy.
  • Know the first reference: T. Eberhart is a spot-up shooter, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: L. Leite, T. Dias are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are B. Ferreira and M. Newman; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with L. Leite, T. Dias, and J. Embalo; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from B. Ferreira and M. Newman; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially B. Ferreira, M. Newman, and T. Eberhart; force them away from preferred areas.
