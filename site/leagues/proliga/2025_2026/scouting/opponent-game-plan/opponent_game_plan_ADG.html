How They Play

Tempo
League-average pace; rhythm control matters more than pure speed against them.
Ball Movement
Ball movement is close to league average; they can share it but are not purely pass-first.
Offensive Glass
Second-chance threat; the first stop is not enough against this team.
Turnovers
Turnover profile is close to league average; pressure helps, but it is not the whole plan.
Physicality
Foul profile sits near league average; physicality is part of the game, but not their defining trait.
Shot Mix
Above-average three-point volume; spacing discipline and clean closeouts matter.

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Own the rhythm: pace itself is not the main separator, so the quality of possessions matters more than speed.
  • Finish possessions: the first stop is not enough against this team because the offensive glass is a real source of extra points.
  • Stay connected to spacing: their perimeter volume can bend the floor if your help and recovery are sloppy.
  • Know the first reference: L. Black is a paint finisher, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.

Scouting Notes

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are A. Guedes and H. Semedo; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with R. Rousseau, J. Cardoso, and N. Ferrar; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from J. Conceicao and H. Semedo; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially R. Soares, A. Guedes, and L. Black; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: A. Guedes | H. Semedo | J. Conceicao | L. Black | R. Rousseau.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: A. Guedes | H. Semedo | J. Cardoso | R. Soares | T. Nazario.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: A. Guedes | H. Semedo and A. Guedes | H. Semedo | L. Black.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Second-chance threat; the first stop is not enough against this team.
  • Do not lose R. Rousseau and J. Cardoso in help-and-recover situations.
