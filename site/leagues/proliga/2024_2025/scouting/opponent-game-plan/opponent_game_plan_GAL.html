Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Control the rhythm: they are more comfortable in a controlled half-court game, so do not let them play cleanly into structure.
  • Win the first action: they do not consistently hurt teams with second-chance volume, so clean first stops carry value.
  • Know the first reference: B. Fitzgerald is a inside scorer, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: M. Bastos, A. Sousa are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are A. Sousa and F. Albergaria; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with M. Bastos, A. Sousa, and A. Brandao; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from J. JR and F. Albergaria; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Above the Break 3; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially A. Sousa, F. Albergaria, and J. JR; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: A. Sousa | F. Albergaria | J. JR | M. Bastos | T. Arikawe.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: A. Sousa | J. JR | M. Bastos | T. Arikawe | V. Silva.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: A. Sousa | J. JR and A. Sousa | J. JR | M. Bastos.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Limited offensive rebounding impact; finishing the first contest usually kills the possession.
  • Do not lose M. Bastos and A. Sousa in help-and-recover situations.
