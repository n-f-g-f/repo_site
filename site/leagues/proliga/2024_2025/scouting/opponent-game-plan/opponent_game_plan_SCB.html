How They Play

Game Plan in 4 Keys

  • Set the tempo early: if you let them run into flow, the game can speed up on their terms.
  • Finish possessions: the first stop is not enough against this team because the offensive glass is a real source of extra points.
  • Know the first reference: J. II is a paint finisher, and the plan should start with taking away his comfort.
  • Closeout discipline matters: J. Lucas, L. Banora are the first perimeter names you cannot lose in help situations.

Scouting Notes

Scouting Notes

Offense

  • The main offensive references are S. Verde and T. Simao; load the scouting plan into those names first.

Perimeter Shooting

  • Priority closeouts start with J. Lucas, L. Banora, and E. Samuco; do not lose them in help-and-recover situations.
  • Some perimeter volume comes from S. Verde and L. Faial; contest, but do not over-help into secondary advantages.

Scoring Areas

  • Their offense naturally flows toward Restricted Area; protect that area early in possessions.
  • Several players have clear comfort zones, especially D. Tchoua, S. Verde, and K. Tchoua; force them away from preferred areas.

Units

  • Most-used lineup to rehearse first: D. Tchoua | J. II | J. Taylor | L. Banora | S. Verde.
  • Secondary lineup to recognize: J. Bingham II | K. Tchoua | L. Faial | S. Verde | T. Simao.
  • Recognize the main duo/trio combinations: J. Taylor | S. Verde and D. Tchoua | J. II | S. Verde.

Defensive Emphasis

  • Second-chance threat; the first stop is not enough against this team.
  • Do not lose J. Lucas and L. Banora in help-and-recover situations.
